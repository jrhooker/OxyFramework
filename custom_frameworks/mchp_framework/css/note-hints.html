different kinds of notes

important notes

ANSI Z535.4 2011 controlled. Caution is used for hazardous situations that, if not avoided, might cause an injury that is classified as minor to moderate.
ANSI Z535.4 2011 controlled. A Warning is the right choice when there is a serious hazard that could result in injury or death.
ANSI Z535.4 2011 controlled. Danger is used for situations where there is a specific hazard present that will cause a serious injury or even death if people don't actively avoid it. The danger signal word should only be used in the most serious situations.
ANSI Z535.4 2011 controlled. Notice is used for signs that convey other information when there is no hazard present and no risk for personal injury. Can be used for instructions, to indicate safety procedures, or to alert workers of the location of safety equipment. In our documentation, this is used for risk of property damage. Caution is reserved for risk of physical injury.
Generic note. Just a general method of calling out a specific fact outside of the main flow of the document.
Attention. Use when there is the possibility of damage to equipment or other non-living object.
Fastpath. Use to point out the existence of shortcuts or the fastest way of accomplishing the task being discussed.
Important. A statement which is important to quality and performance. It can precede or come immediately after its associated section.
Remember. Use this to remind the reader of an import point about the current subject which was made in an earlier topic.
Restriction. Use for drawing attention to usage restrictions related to the subject in question.
Tip. A statement consisting of a helpful hint or tip for the reader. It usually follows immediately after its associated section.
Trouble. Troubleshooting information. Use to direct attention to the common mistakes.
